Core Principles of AI Guidance

AI is a tool that can help with research, writing, and planning. It is not a thinking being, a replacement for teachers, or always accurate. All AI tools must go through the ERMA (Enterprise Request Management Application) process before interacting with student data.

The Traffic Light Framework

NYCPS names what AI will never be allowed to do before naming what it is allowed to do.

Red: Do Not Proceed

These uses are completely off-limits. They represent the highest risk to students, families, and fairness.

  • •Decisions About Students (Placement, discipline, grading, graduation)
  • •IEP and 504 Plan Development
  • •Assessments and Grading
  • •Surveillance and Behavior Monitoring
  • •Care and Counseling (Crisis intervention)
  • •Deciding a Child's Course Path
  • •Training AI Models with Student Data

Yellow: Caution

Allowed, but only with careful thought and strong adult oversight. Human review is mandatory.

  • •Student and School Data Analysis
  • •Drafting Critical Communications (Needs human linguist review)
  • •Translations for Diverse Learners (Scaffolds, bilingual materials)
  • •Student Use of AI (Needs educator guidance and age-appropriate context)

Green: Confidence

Approved and supported uses to help staff do their jobs more effectively.

  • •Brainstorming and Organizing lesson ideas
  • •Drafting and Refining non-sensitive communications
  • •Simplifying and Streamlining operational tasks
  • •Non-critical Translation (with AI disclosure if not human-reviewed)
  • •Accessibility material creation
  • •Educator Professional Learning

Age-Appropriate AI Guidance

Elementary (K-5)

Students are learning ABOUT AI. Use should be supervised. Focus on asking questions and explaining that AI is a tool, not a replacement for thinking.

Middle School (6-8)

Students are learning WITH AI. Transition to some independence with clear boundaries. Discuss academic honesty and how to verify information.

High School (9-12)

Support mature, responsible use. Discuss citing AI assistance. Emphasize critical evaluation of outputs and academic integrity.

5 Questions to Ask Teachers

  • 1 How are you using AI in the classroom?
  • 2 What AI tools are approved for my child to use?
  • 3 When is it okay for my child to use AI for homework?
  • 4 How should my child cite or acknowledge AI use?
  • 5 What should I do if I have concerns about AI use?

Family Do's and Don'ts

Do: Talk with your child about protecting personal info.
Don't: Assume AI tools automatically protect privacy.
Do: Speak regularly about how/why they use AI for school.
Don't: Wait until there is a problem to talk about AI use.
Do: Ask your child to explain their thinking in their own words.
Don't: Replace your child's thinking or creativity with AI.
Do: Talk about what AI helps with and where it falls short.
Don't: Assume AI responses are neutral or always correct.
